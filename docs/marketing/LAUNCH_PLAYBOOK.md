## 2. Account Creation Order

The sequence matters. Easier-to-claim platforms first builds momentum and confirms the handle is genuinely available before you commit elsewhere. Cross-linking happens AFTER all four profiles exist — link prematurely and you risk pointing followers at half-built profiles.

### Sequence + rationale

**Step 1 — YouTube (Google account).** ~30 minutes.
Why first: YouTube uses Google account auth, the most stable signup flow. If the handle is taken here, you reroute to the fallback before claiming elsewhere. YouTube also takes the longest to populate (banner, channel description, trailer upload), so starting here lets background uploads finish while you claim the other three.

**Step 2 — Instagram (brand account).** ~25 minutes.
Why second: Instagram's claim flow is fast. Once Step 1 confirms `@teammyrx` is available, claim it here immediately to prevent same-day squatting. The IG account is what Threads will sync to in Tier 2 month-2, so getting the brand account right matters more than the personal accounts.

**Step 3 — LinkedIn personal profile updates + company page creation.** ~45 minutes.
Why third: LinkedIn company pages require an existing personal profile that's been active. Update Motaz's personal LinkedIn first (banner, headline, About section), then create the MyRX company page from that profile. Company-page verification can take 24 hours — submit before noon so it's processing while you finish other channels.

**Step 4 — X (Twitter) personal account update.** ~20 minutes.
Why last: X is the lowest-time-cost setup of the four and the personal account already exists in some form. Update bio, banner, pinned tweet. The first thread launches separately on Day 1.

### Cross-link timing

DO NOT cross-link bios until all four profiles are complete with banner, picture, full bio, and at least one piece of content. A profile linking to a stub is a credibility hole the first 100 visitors will fall through.

Order of cross-linking once all profiles are live:
1. Instagram bio gets the YouTube link first (the proof surface a coach checks).
2. YouTube channel description gets the Instagram link + the LinkedIn link.
3. LinkedIn personal profile gets the YouTube link in the "Featured" section.
4. X bio gets the YouTube link (single link slot — pick the strongest proof surface).

### First-post stagger by platform optimal-time

Don't post all four at once. Stagger by each platform's research-backed optimal posting window so the first impression hits when each audience is most likely to see it.

- **LinkedIn:** Tuesday 8:30 AM local time. Coach decision-makers check LinkedIn over morning coffee.
- **Instagram:** Same Tuesday, 12:30 PM. Lunch-break scroll.
- **YouTube:** Same Tuesday, 5:00 PM. Evening watch window starts.
- **X:** Same Tuesday, 11:00 AM. Mid-morning desk break — when X engagement peaks for evidence-based fitness accounts.

If Launch Day is a different weekday, shift to the same time slots. If it's a weekend, push to Tuesday — weekday discovery for B2B/coach acquisition outperforms weekend by 3-4x on LinkedIn.

## 5. Cross-Linking Order

Wait until all four profiles are complete (banner, picture, bio, at least one post) before linking any of them to the others. A premature link points followers at a half-built profile.

### Linking sequence (do in this order)

1. **Instagram bio** → add the YouTube link in the website slot (single-link platform — YouTube is the proof surface a coach checks).
2. **YouTube channel description** → add the Instagram link and the LinkedIn company-page link (YouTube allows multiple).
3. **LinkedIn personal profile Featured section** → add the YouTube link as a featured media tile.
4. **LinkedIn company page** → add Instagram link in the "Featured" section, YouTube link in the website-fields block.
5. **X bio** → add the YouTube link (single slot — pick the strongest proof surface).

### Bottom-of-bio "find us elsewhere" pattern

For platforms that allow multiple links (LinkedIn, YouTube), end the bio/description with a compact one-liner:

> "Also on Instagram (@teammyrx), YouTube (@teammyrx), and X (@motazjarrah)."

Keep the syntax identical across channels — the visual repetition signals brand consistency to the user landing on multiple profiles in the same session.

## 7. Post-Launch 24-Hour Watch

The first 24 hours after a launch post drive 60-80% of its total reach. Active engagement during that window is the single highest-leverage activity of Launch Day.

### Engagement to monitor

- **LinkedIn:** Comments on the long-form post, connection requests with notes referencing the post, DMs to Motaz's personal inbox, mentions in other coaches' feeds.
- **Instagram:** Comments on the carousel, DMs, story mentions, saves (saves are the strongest 2026 signal — track them).
- **YouTube:** Comments on the trailer, channel subscriptions, watch-time on the trailer (aim for 50%+ retention on a 60-90 sec video).
- **X:** Replies to the thread, retweets with comment, mentions of `@teammyrx`, likes from accounts you recognize as industry voices.

### When to reply (first 2 hours critical)

- **Reply to every comment within the first 2 hours** of each post going live. Speed-of-reply is the strongest algorithm signal across LinkedIn, Instagram, and YouTube in 2026.
- **First 24 hours:** Reply within 4 hours of any new comment or DM. After 24 hours, daily replies are fine.
- **3-pillar voice in every reply.** Even a 3-sentence reply acknowledges the commenter's state, names the mechanism behind their question, and points to a next step. Don't drop the voice in replies — the audience is reading them.

### When NOT to engage

- **Trolls and bad-faith comments:** Don't argue. Ignore or delete. Engagement boosts visibility — never boost a hostile comment.
- **Spam / link-drop comments:** Delete and block. LinkedIn and Instagram both penalize accounts that leave spam comments visible.
- **Out-of-scope healthcare / medical advice questions:** Polite redirect to "Talk to your doctor or a registered dietitian — MyRX is a fitness coaching platform, not a medical service." Don't get drawn into specific medical guidance. This is the Health & Medical Disclaimer in practice.
- **Competitor mentions:** Don't punch down. If someone compares MyRX to Trainerize or TrueCoach, acknowledge what those tools do well and explain the MyRX mechanism difference (next-step algorithmic prescription vs custom-program builder). Never trash a competitor by name.

### Direct messages

- **Coach DMs asking about the product:** Respond personally within 4 hours. Send them the coach signup link with the trial mentioned ("14 days free, no card required for the trial").
- **Athlete DMs asking about pricing:** Send them to myrxfit.com/pricing. Don't pitch in DM — the page is designed to convert.
- **Press / podcast DMs:** Acknowledge, propose a 30-min call. Use Calendly if you have one, otherwise propose two specific time slots.

### End-of-day-1 review

Before signing off Day 1, spend 30 minutes reviewing:

1. Which post got the most engagement? (Note the format — carousel vs Reel vs long-form vs thread.)
2. What's the most common question in comments/DMs?
3. Are there any factual errors anyone called out? Fix immediately.
4. Are any platform accounts showing "limited reach" or shadow-ban indicators? (LinkedIn: posts getting <50 impressions in first hour. X: thread engagement collapsing after tweet 1. Address by reviewing platform-policy compliance — usually external-link density is the culprit.)

Save the questions from #2 — those become the next week's content calendar. The audience has just told you what they want to see next.
