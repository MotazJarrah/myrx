# Posting cadence + timing — 2026 research

Research date: 2026-05-29. Audience: fitness coaching B2B2C (coaches subscribing $19-99/mo) and B2C (athletes subscribing $4.99-6.99/mo). Team size: 1-2 people. Position: calm-coach voice (between RP/SBS/Trainerize/Strava).

---

## TL;DR — what we'll actually do

| Platform | Realistic cadence (1-2 person team) | Anchor times (ET, weekdays unless noted) | Account type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | 3-4 posts/wk (founder), 1-2/wk (brand) | Tue-Thu 10-11am, secondary 12-2pm | Founder-led, brand mirrors |
| **Instagram** | 3 Reels + 2 carousels/wk = 5 posts/wk | Weekday 6-8am OR 5-7pm; Tue/Wed best | Brand-led (@teammyrx), founder mirrors stories |
| **YouTube** | 1 long-form/wk + 3 Shorts/wk | Long-form drops Tue/Thu 3-5pm; Shorts daily mid-day | Brand-led, founder hosts |
| **X (Twitter)** | 1-2 tweets/day + 1 thread/wk | Spread 7am, 12pm, 5pm; thread Tue/Wed 9-11am | Founder-led primary |

Total weekly output: ~15-18 pieces. Sustainable for a 1-2 person team if Reels and Shorts share footage and threads/carousels share source ideas.

---

## Per-platform deep dive

### LinkedIn — founder-led, mid-week mornings

**Cadence reality (2026):** The "post daily" era is over. Algorithm throttles same-day duplicates. Sweet spot for founders: 3-4 high-quality posts/wk. Brand pages: 2-3/wk max — and only as supporting infrastructure, not the growth engine.

**Best times:** Tue-Thu 10-11am audience local time. Secondary window 12-2pm for B2B SaaS (lunch-break scroll). Tuesday strongest single day for tech/software vertical.

**ROI for fitness coaches (B2B2C):** Worth the time if coach subscription is the primary revenue. LinkedIn lead CPL runs 28% below Google Ads and converts at ~2x rate, but only for deal sizes >$5,000 and sales cycles >30 days. Our coach tiers ($189-989/yr) sit at the lower edge of that band — LinkedIn works, but needs to be founder-led and content-first (not outreach-spam). Personal profiles get 8x the engagement and 561% more reach than identical content on company pages.

**Decision:** Founder account (Motaz) is the primary LinkedIn surface. Brand page exists as receipt-of-existence — drops 1-2 posts/wk mirroring the founder's best.

### Instagram — brand-led for the B2C funnel

**Cadence reality (2026):** 3-4 Reels/wk + 2 carousels/wk + occasional static is the optimal mix. 10+ posts/wk burns the team and hits diminishing returns. Carousels are the engagement winner at ~10% vs Reels ~6% and static ~7%. Optimal carousel: 6-13 slides. Optimal Reel: 30-90 sec educational, 7-15 sec for trend-jumping.

**Best times:** Fitness vertical peaks 5-8am (pre-workout planning) and 5-7pm (post-work). Weekends 8-10am and 4-6pm. Tue/Wed strongest for health-conscious audience.

**Algorithm signals that actually matter:** Watch time, likes-per-reach, sends-per-reach. Sends are the #1 signal — content that gets sent to friends spreads to Explore.

**Decision:** @teammyrx is brand-led (visual coaching content sells the athlete experience). Founder appears in some Reels and Stories but isn't the protagonist. Mirrors the Trainerize playbook: the platform is the star, the founder is the credibility layer.

### YouTube — depth-of-trust play, slow burn

**Cadence reality (2026):** 1-2 long-form videos/wk + 3-5 Shorts/wk for channels under 100K subs. Creators using BOTH formats grow 3x faster than single-format. Long-form turns Shorts viewers into session time and converts to subscription. Don't commit to a cadence you can't sustain — consistent weekly beats sporadic daily.

**Best times:** Long-form drops Tue/Thu afternoon 3-5pm catch the after-work watch window. Shorts work mid-day, daily if footage exists.

**Realistic for 1-2 person team:** 1 long-form/wk (filmed in batches) + 3 Shorts/wk (cut from long-form footage). Shorts test which topics land; long-form goes deep on the winners.

**Decision:** Brand channel hosts the videos, founder is the on-camera host. Naming: MyRX (not @teammyrx — YouTube treats brand name differently).

### X — founder voice, builder-in-public

**Cadence reality (2026):** 2-3 quality tweets/day for established accounts; 3-5/day when starting. 1-2 threads/wk (4-8 tweets each, with visuals). First 30 min engagement velocity is THE single biggest distribution factor — early replies/likes determine algorithmic reach.

**Best times:** Spread across day — 7am (morning scroll), 12pm (lunch), 5pm (commute). Threads land best Tue/Wed 9-11am.

**Founder vs brand:** Builder-in-public is the dominant founder X playbook in 2026. Sharing the real journey (wins, losses, decisions, metrics) outperforms polished brand copy. Reply strategy matters as much as posting — 30 min/day replying to bigger accounts drives more profile visits than 5 original posts.

**Decision:** Founder-led primary. @teammyrx exists as a redirect (bio links to founder). Builder-in-public threads document the coach platform launch, athlete milestone moments, the calm-coach philosophy.

## Founder vs brand account — the data settles it

Across every Tier-1 platform, personal profiles outperform brand pages in 2026:
- LinkedIn: 5-7x more organic reach, 8x more engagement
- All platforms: algorithms favor people over logos
- Trust: 82% of consumers trust companies more when CEO engages publicly; 77% more likely to buy

For a 1-2 person team launching a new product, founder-led isn't optional — it's the only growth path that works without paid amplification. Brand accounts exist to validate the company is real (basic search hygiene) and host long-form assets (YouTube videos, Instagram visual library).

**Split: 70% founder, 30% brand.** Founder drives top-of-funnel growth. Brand catches the validation traffic.

---

## Consistency vs quality tradeoff (2026 verdict)

Every platform's algorithm rewards quality over volume in 2026. The "post 10x/day" advice is dead — it triggers throttling. The actual hierarchy:

1. Consistency over volume — 3 posts/wk for a year beats 10 posts/wk for one month
2. Quality over consistency — one excellent post weekly beats five mediocre daily
3. Format-native quality — a great Reel is different from a great LinkedIn post; cross-posting hurts on every platform

Practical commitment for our team: 5 days/wk operating cadence, weekends off. Aspirational ceiling, not minimum.
