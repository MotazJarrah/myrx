## Asset specs (per `_research/specs.md`)

### Profile picture — `profile_picture.png`
- **Dimensions:** 1200 x 1200 px (recommended), 400 x 400 minimum
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 (square; LinkedIn crops to a circle on display)
- **Format:** PNG (preferred for sharp edges on brand marks) or JPG
- **Max file size:** 8 MB
- **Safe zone:** Center circle. Anything in the outer 10% of the square crops off.
- **Recommended content:** Headshot of Motaz, dark background (matches MyRX palette `#131A17`), lime accent (`#CAF240`) if any branding overlay. No text — LinkedIn renders profile pictures at 152x152 in the feed; text won't read.

### Banner — `banner.png`
- **Dimensions:** 1584 x 396 px
- **Aspect ratio:** 4:1
- **Format:** PNG or JPG (animated GIF supported but visual stability serves brand better)
- **Max file size:** 8 MB
- **Safe zone:** Avoid the **bottom-left 568 x 264 px** region — that's where the profile photo overlays on both mobile and desktop. The right 70% and the top half are safe for the wordmark, tagline, or CTA.
- **Recommended content:**
  - Background: Dark surface (`#191F1C`) or DARK (`#131A17`)
  - MyRX wordmark (the no-slogan version per the brand rule — only the signup welcome screen uses the slogan wordmark) on the right side
  - Subtagline near the wordmark: "Performance Lab." in lime (`#CAF240`)
  - Optional: One-line value prop in FG (`#F4F3EF`), e.g. "The algorithm picks the next step. The coach oversees."

### Featured / first post image — `first_post_image.png`
- **Dimensions:** 1200 x 1200 px (square) — best in-feed performance
- **Alternative:** 1200 x 1500 px (4:5 portrait) also performs well in 2026
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 or 4:5
- **Format:** PNG or JPG
- **Safe zone:** Center; LinkedIn shows the full image in feed without crop at these ratios.
- **Recommended content:** A single-slide methodology snapshot mirroring the launch post's three-bullet block. Dark background, lime accent on the headline ("Next step. Every time."), three short methodology lines below it. Tagline "Performance Lab." in the bottom-right corner.

## Hashtag strategy (per `_research/hashtags.md`)

LinkedIn 2026 rule: **3 hashtags per post.** Algorithm rewards niche professional tags; below 3 means no classification, above 3 reads as spammy.

The slot formula for every post:

| Slot | Purpose | Default tag |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Niche broad | `#strengthcoach` OR `#hybridathlete` OR `#enduranceCoach` (pick by post topic) |
| 2 | Audience | `#onlinecoaching` (B2B coach) OR `#mastersathlete` (B2C consumer) |
| 3 | Brand | `#myrxfit` (claim early — low-volume brandable tags compound over years) |

When a post leans into a specific methodology, swap one of the broad slots for an exact-topic tag:
- `#polarizedtraining`, `#zone2training`, `#vo2max` (cardio methodology posts)
- `#progressiveoverload`, `#1rmcalculator`, `#rir2` (strength methodology posts)
- `#mifflinstjeor`, `#tdee` (calorie methodology posts)

## What makes the voice land on LinkedIn specifically

LinkedIn rewards depth over hook gimmickry. Where Instagram's hook has to grab in the first second of a Reel and X's hook has to fit in 280 characters, LinkedIn's hook has to **earn the click on "See more"** — the first 210 chars of a long-form post determine whether the algorithm shows the rest to anyone.

Format rules that follow from that:

1. **Lead with insight, not introduction.** The first sentence is a contrarian observation, a surprising number, or an uncomfortable truth. No "I'm excited to share…" No "I've been thinking about…" The reader is mid-scroll; the first line either earns the next line or doesn't.

2. **The 3-pillar shape fits the format natively.** Acknowledge the coach's current state (line 1-3), explain the mechanism (the body), name the realistic next step (close). LinkedIn's algorithm rewards posts that start a thread of substantive coach comments — that means the close should leave room for a coach to add their own data point, not telegraph a "what do you think?" prompt.

3. **Mechanism specificity is the moat.** Where other platforms penalize technical depth, LinkedIn rewards it. Naming the formulas (Epley, Brzycki, Lombardi, Riegel, Mifflin-St Jeor, ACSM activity multipliers) is the credibility play. Hand-waving costs trust here.

4. **No emojis on platform copy.** Per the brand rule. LinkedIn-native communication uses em-dashes for thought breaks. Emojis read as performative on this surface in 2026.

5. **Carousels beat text posts for methodology breakdowns.** A 6-10 slide carousel walking a coach through one piece of the next-step thesis (how the algorithm picks a barbell next-target, how the polarized 80/20 queue is generated, how the calorie realism factor is calibrated) outperforms a 1,500-char text post on the same topic. Save carousels for the methodology pillar; use text for the contrarian one-liner.

6. **The brand mid-sentence convention applies.** Per the lock: brand mid-sentence, verb-first in titles. "Building MyRX" in the headline. "I started building MyRX" in the launch post. Never "MyRX — the platform for coaches."

7. **Founder voice > brand voice on LinkedIn.** Personal profiles get 8x the engagement and 561% more reach than identical content on company pages. The brand page mirrors at 1-2 posts/week; the founder account drives growth.
