Most coaches still program by feel. The strongest ones spend hours each week recalculating loads, rewriting macro targets, and chasing client check-ins.

That work doesn't scale. By client 25, the program quality drops or the coach burns out. Usually both.

MyRX takes the calculation work off the coach. The algorithm reads the athlete's logged efforts and prescribes the next weight on every lift, the next pace on every interval, the next macro split for the next phase. The coach reviews, adjusts the parameters, and spends their time on the work that actually moves outcomes — message threads, intake reviews, the one client who needs a real conversation this week.

A few specifics on what's under the hood:

— Strength: Epley + Brzycki + Lombardi averaged, with rep-range-aware drops past 10 reps (Brzycki under-projects).
— Cardio: Riegel pace projection across distances, Daniels' offsets for endurance / threshold / VO2 prescriptions.
— Calories: Mifflin-St Jeor BMR, ACSM activity multipliers, plus a realism factor calibrated against actual loss/gain rates per body-fat band.
— Concept2 ergs: watts derived from pace via Concept2's published formula. Air Bike: cal/min anchored zones with watts floors per zone.
— Health Connect, Samsung Health, Apple HealthKit on the roadmap. Polar is live, Strava and Garmin in flight.

This account is where I share what we're learning while building it — the methodology, the math, the trade-offs that come up when you try to replace ten years of coaching intuition with a model that explains itself.
