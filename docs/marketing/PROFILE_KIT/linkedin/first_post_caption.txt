CAPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Most coaches still pick the next weight by feel.

The strongest ones spend two hours on a Sunday recalculating loads, rewriting macro splits, and reading the same chat threads three times trying to figure out who needs what next.

I've watched that work eat ten-year careers. By client 25, the program quality drops or the coach quits. Usually both.

So I started building MyRX.

The algorithm reads the athlete's logged efforts and prescribes:
— the next weight on every lift (Epley + Brzycki + Lombardi averaged, with rep-range-aware drops past 10)
— the next pace on every interval (Riegel projection across distances, Daniels' offsets for endurance / threshold / VO2)
— the next macro split for the next phase (Mifflin-St Jeor + ACSM activity multipliers + a realism factor calibrated against actual loss rates per body-fat band)

The coach doesn't program. The coach oversees.

You see your roster's next-step state at a glance, adjust the parameters that drive it (calorie pace, weight goal, macro preset, fat-level band), and spend the time you used to lose to spreadsheets on the work that actually moves outcomes — the message thread, the intake review, the one client who needs a real conversation this week.

That's the bet. The algorithm carries the math. The coach carries the relationship.

I'll be sharing what we're learning while we build it — the methodology, the math, the trade-offs that come up when you try to replace ten years of coaching intuition with a model that explains itself.

Coach platform's at myrxfit.com/coach. Athlete app's at myrxfit.com.

Performance Lab.

#strengthcoach #onlinecoaching #myrxfit
