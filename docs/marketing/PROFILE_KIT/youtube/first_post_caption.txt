Most fitness apps log what you already did. MyRX picks what you do next.

Your last set told us something. The algorithm reads it and writes the next prescription — the exact weight for your next set, the exact pace for your next interval, the exact macro target for your next meal. Not a generic plan. Not a template you adjust by feel. The next step, computed from your actual data.

In this video, I walk through the four systems the platform runs:

1. The strength algorithm — how Epley, Brzycki, and Lombardi projections combine to pick the next weight at any rep target.

2. The cardio progression queue — how the polarized 80/20 rule prevents back-to-back hard days and keeps your endurance base intact.

3. The calorie plan — how Mifflin-St Jeor, ACSM activity factors, and a realism factor produce a target you can actually hit.

4. The coach overlay — how a coach oversees the prescription without programming from scratch.

The free tier covers strength, cardio, and mobility. CoreRX adds bodyweight and calories for $4.99/mo (or $49.99/yr). FullRX adds heart rate, hydration, and sleep for $6.99/mo (or $69.99/yr). Coaches subscribe; their athletes train free.
