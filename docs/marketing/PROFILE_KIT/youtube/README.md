# YouTube profile kit — @teammyrx

The depth-of-trust play. The only Tier-1 platform where reach
compounds for years — a video ranking for "polarized training pace
zones" pulls coach and athlete traffic forever. YouTube indexes both
audiences: athletes search "how to fix my bench plateau", coaches
search "how to scale to 30 clients without burnout". Same channel
serves both.

## Account format

Brand-led channel, founder-hosted videos. The channel name is "MyRX —
Performance Lab" (not @teammyrx — YouTube treats brand name
differently from handle). The handle is @teammyrx for cross-platform
consistency.

The platform is the protagonist; the founder is the on-camera host.
This mirrors the Trainerize playbook — the product sells the
experience, the founder appears as the credibility layer. Coaches
researching the platform see a founder explaining the system; they
don't see a faceless brand channel.

The handle `@teammyrx` is the primary. `@myrxfit` is unavailable
(RxFit competitor holds it elsewhere on the web). `@myrxapp` is the
fallback.

## Hashtag strategy

YouTube 2026 rules:
- **Title**: 1 hashtag (the highest-intent niche tag). Title
  hashtags appear ABOVE the video title once published, clickable.
- **Description**: 3-5 hashtags at the end. YouTube caps at 15 but
  the algorithm penalizes anything above 5.

The 4-slot formula for every MyRX video:

1. **1 niche broad** — `#strengthcoach` OR `#hybridathlete` OR
   `#endurancecoach`
2. **1-2 exact-topic** — matches the specific video subject
   (`#zone2training`, `#progressiveoverload`, `#csscalculator`,
   `#polarizedtraining`)
3. **1 audience** — who the video is for
   (`#onlinecoaching` for the B2B coach audience, `#mastersathlete`
   for B2C consumers)
4. **1 brand** — `#PerformanceLab` (always; brand tag compounds
   over years)

The title hashtag is always `#PerformanceLab` — highest brand-
recall, owns the search result.

**Banned tags** (zero ranking signal in 2026, crowd out the niche
tags that DO carry signal):
- `#fyp`, `#foryou`, `#viral`, `#subscribe`
- `#fitness`, `#gym`, `#workout`
- `#fitnessmotivation`, `#fitfam`
- Any tag above ~500K videos

Sweet spot: 10K-500K videos per tag.

## Chapters — non-negotiable on every video > 4 minutes

YouTube videos with **3+ chapter markers** see an **11% average
watch-time uplift** (YouTube 2026 creator data). Chapters with
keyword-rich titles get indexed by Google as **Key Moments** — they
can appear above standard search results.

Rules:
- First chapter MUST be `0:00`.
- Format: `MM:SS — Title` (em-dash separator, not hyphen).
- Each chapter title written as a Google search query — "what would
  someone search to land on JUST this section?"
- Minimum 3 chapters per video over 4 minutes.

Example from the launch video:

```
0:00 — What the next-step thesis means
1:30 — How the strength algorithm picks your next weight
4:00 — How the polarized cardio queue avoids back-to-back hard days
6:45 — How the calorie plan adapts when the scale stops moving
9:15 — How a coach oversees the prescription
11:00 — How to get started
```

Each of those 6 strings is a real search query. Each indexes
independently as a Google Key Moment.

## Posting cadence

1 long-form per week + 3 Shorts per week.

- **Long-form**: Tuesday or Thursday, 3-5 PM ET. Catches the after-
  work watch window.
- **Shorts**: Daily-ish, mid-day, strongest Tue-Thu. Cut from the
  long-form footage of the week — never net-new production.
- **Weekend**: One Short Saturday or Sunday if a strong cut exists.
  Otherwise skip; the algorithm rewards weekday consistency on
  fitness channels.

The math: 1 long-form filmed in batches + 3 Shorts cut from the
same footage = ~15 hours/week sustained.

## Algorithm signals that actually move reach

In rank order — optimize the top, not the bottom:

1. **Watch time** — total minutes watched, weighted heaviest. A
   12-minute video with 60% completion beats a 4-minute video with
   90% completion on absolute watch time.
2. **Click-through rate (CTR)** — thumbnail + title combo. The
   threshold for "video is working" is ~4% CTR; above 8% the
   algorithm starts pushing it hard.
3. **Average view duration** — viewers staying past the first 30
   seconds is the make-or-break signal. Hook hard in the first 10.
4. **Session time** — does a viewer keep watching MyRX content after
   this video? Chapters and end screens that point at related
   videos compound this.
5. **Subscribes per view** — secondary but signals long-term value.

Critical: Shorts and long-form have DIFFERENT ranking systems.
Don't treat Shorts performance as a long-form proxy.

## What makes the voice land on YouTube specifically

Three things, in order:

### 1. Thumbnail + title combo carries the hook
YouTube's discovery is visual-first. Viewers see the thumbnail
before they see anything else. The thumbnail text and the title
have to work together — neither alone carries the click. For the
launch video: thumbnail says "Your next step. / Computed." (state +
mechanism). Title says "How MyRX Picks Your Next Weight, Pace, and
Macro" (specifics + brand). Together they name the value before the
viewer clicks.

### 2. Chapters ARE the 3-pillar structure
Long-form on YouTube gives the 3-pillar voice room to breathe at
the depth that builds trust. Open with the current state the viewer
is in (acknowledge), walk through the mechanism with specifics and
real numbers (mechanism), end on the concrete next step (next
step). Each chapter is one pillar OR one mechanism component. The
chapter list IS the table of contents for the voice.

### 3. The description first 200 chars do the SEO work
YouTube indexes the first 200 chars of a description as the primary
search signal. The launch description opens with "Most fitness apps
log what you already did. MyRX picks what you do next." — 79 chars,
primary keyword "picks what you do next" lands in char 35-60. The
contrarian framing AND the keyword phrase both land before YouTube
truncates the description in search previews.

## Long-form vs Shorts — different playbooks

**Long-form (8-12 min):**
- Founder-hosted, on-camera.
- Chapters mandatory.
- 3-pillar structure spans the whole video.
- Goal: trust + session time.

**Shorts (60-90 sec):**
- Single mechanism explainer.
- Hook in the first 1-2 seconds.
- One specific number, one specific takeaway.
- Goal: discovery + funnel viewers to long-form.

Shorts are cut from long-form footage 80% of the time. Each long-
form filming day produces 3-4 Shorts as byproduct. Don't write
Shorts as net-new scripts.
