PRIMARY BIO LINK
================

myrxfit.com

This is a single-link setup. Instagram's bio supports one clickable link
and the homepage already routes the two audiences correctly — athletes
land on the app download, coaches land on the coach signup CTA.

A Linktree-style page is NOT recommended at launch. Reasons:
- Adds a tap between the bio click and the conversion surface.
- Fragments analytics across two domains.
- Linktree pages don't carry the brand voice — landing on a third-party
  link aggregator after a polished IG profile breaks channel coherence.
- myrxfit.com already handles both audiences with the same coach-led
  voice. No reason to route through a list.

Revisit Linktree (or a self-hosted /links page on myrxfit.com) once
there are 5+ destinations worth surfacing at once (app, coach signup,
latest YouTube, newsletter signup, podcast, community). At launch,
the homepage IS the link.

WHEN TO ROTATE THE LINK
=======================

Keep myrxfit.com as the default. Rotate temporarily for:
- New YouTube long-form drop → swap to the video URL for 48 hours, then back.
- Newsletter launch → swap to the subscribe page for 7 days.
- Coach pricing reveal → swap to /coach/pricing for the launch week.

Always return to myrxfit.com within 14 days. The link is a conversion
surface, not a content surface.

IF A LINKTREE-STYLE PAGE IS BUILT LATER
========================================

Use these labels and order. Each label follows the seven voice rules —
verb-first, no filler, lowercase "coach" and "athlete" in body copy.

1. Get the app — iOS and Android
   → app.myrxfit.com (or store badges)

2. Become a coach — start a free trial
   → myrxfit.com/coach/signup

3. How MyRX picks your next step
   → myrxfit.com/how-it-works

4. Latest from the channel
   → youtube.com/@teammyrx

5. Pricing — athletes and coaches
   → myrxfit.com/pricing

6. Read the science behind the algorithm
   → myrxfit.com/methodology

DO NOT use labels like:
- "Click here"
- "Learn more"
- "Sign up now!"
- "Join the journey"
- "Welcome to MyRX"
- "Unlock your potential"

Every label names what's on the other side. Verb first when there's an
action, plain noun when it's reference content. No exclamation points.
