[TWEET 1 / 7] — hook (acknowledge state)

Most fitness apps log what you did.

A few prescribe a plan you have to interpret.

Almost none answer the only question the athlete actually
asks between sets:

what should I do next?


[TWEET 2 / 7] — sharpen the gap

The strength athlete knows the rep range.
The cardio athlete knows the zone target.
The athlete cutting knows the calorie ceiling.

What they don't know is the specific number for THIS set,
THIS interval, THIS meal.

That gap is where most plans break.


[TWEET 3 / 7] — mechanism (the lab thesis)

So we built MyRX around one rule:

every screen names the next step.

Next weight. Next pace. Next macro target. Anchored to the
athlete's actual logged data, not a generic template.

Performance Lab. Not a tracker.


[TWEET 4 / 7] — mechanism (algorithm + coach division of labor)

The algorithm picks the number.

The coach oversees — reviews the trajectory, adjusts the
underlying parameters (calorie pace, weight goal, macro
preset, zone targets), and messages the athlete when something
needs a conversation.

The coach doesn't program from scratch. The athlete doesn't
guess.


[TWEET 5 / 7] — mechanism (why this division matters)

This is the actual lever:

a coach running 30 athletes can't write 30 custom programs
every week. So most don't — they reuse templates.

When the algorithm handles next-step prescription, the coach's
time moves to where it compounds: reading the athlete's full
picture, catching plateaus early, adjusting the plan inputs.


[TWEET 6 / 7] — next step for athletes

If you're an athlete who wants the next step decided for you,
the app is at myrxfit.com.

Free tier covers strength, cardio, mobility.
CoreRX adds body comp + macros.
FullRX adds heart rate, hydration, sleep.

Monthly or annual. 14-day free trial.


[TWEET 7 / 7] — next step for coaches + ask

If you're a coach who wants to run your roster on a next-step
engine instead of a template builder, /coach/signup at myrxfit
walks through it. 14-day trial.

Following along here for the build. Reply with what you'd want
to see broken down first.
