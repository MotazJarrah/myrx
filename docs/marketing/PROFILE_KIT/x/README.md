## Hashtag strategy on X

| Rule | Reasoning |
|---|---|
| **1–2 niche hashtags max per post** | X's 2026 algorithm penalizes hashtag-heavy posts harder than under-tagged ones. Most high-performing X accounts use 0–2. |
| **Use them only on launch posts and major announcements** | Daily posts and replies should stay hashtag-free. Hashtags on X read as advertising — they suppress reach in the For You feed. |
| **Always niche, never broad** | `#fitness` (600M+ posts elsewhere, useless on X). Use `#strengthcoach`, `#hybridathlete`, `#zone2training`, `#polarizedtraining` — the audiences that already read mechanism-first content. |
| **Pinned thread = the exception** | The launch thread uses `#strengthcoach #hybridathlete` on the final tweet. This is the one spot where category tags help discovery. |

Tag set rotation for major announcements:
- Coach-targeted: `#strengthcoach`, `#onlinecoaching`, `#coachingbusiness`
- Athlete-targeted: `#hybridathlete`, `#hyrox`, `#polarizedtraining`, `#zone2training`
- Methodology: `#progressiveoverload`, `#rpe9`, `#vo2max`, `#lactatethreshold`

Pick 1 from coach OR athlete bucket + 1 from methodology. Never
both audiences in one post — confused targeting tanks
distribution.

## Posting cadence (from `_research/timing.md`)

| Activity | Frequency | Best window (US ET) |
|---|---|---|
| Original posts | 2-3/day | Spread 7am, 12pm, 5pm |
| Threads | 1-2/week | Tue/Wed 9-11am |

**Why 2-3 quality posts beats 5+ posts/day in 2026:** X's
algorithm weights first-30-minute engagement velocity above
total daily volume. A post that gets 10 replies in the first
half hour outperforms five posts with one reply each. Quality
density wins.

**Reply strategy matters as much as posting:**
30 min/day replying to Greg Nuckols, Andy Galpin, Stronger by
Science, Eric Helms, Layne Norton, Andrew Huberman (for
fitness-adjacent threads) drives more profile visits than
posting 5 originals. The audience that converts for both arms
of the business lives in those reply threads.

Posting frequency by week:
- **Week 1 (launch):** 3-5 posts/day to build initial signal
- **Week 2+:** Drop to 2-3 posts/day + 1-2 threads/week
- Builder-in-public moments (shipping a feature, hitting a
  milestone, catching a hard bug) get standalone posts

## What makes the voice land on X specifically

X rewards a tight set of mechanics. The 3-pillar voice translates
directly into them:

### 1. The first line IS the post
Threads get clicked if tweet 1 is sharp. Posts get expanded if
the first line earns the click. Lead with the contrarian
observation OR the specific number — never with throat-clearing.

✓ "Most fitness apps log what you did." (sharp observation)
✓ "The algorithm picks the next number; the coach picks the
  parameters." (mechanism stated upfront)
✗ "I've been thinking a lot about how athletes..."
  (hedging — dead on arrival)

### 2. Thread structure mirrors the 3-pillar voice cleanly
Tweets 1-2: acknowledge state (the problem the reader knows).
Tweets 3-5: mechanism (the system that solves it).
Tweets 6-7: next step (where to go from here).

This is the SAME shape as a coaching cue. The voice doesn't
need to flex — the thread shape is the voice in long form.

### 3. Replies do the work of follow-up posts
Don't bury follow-up thoughts in the original thread. Drop
them as replies to your own thread when fresh data lands or
someone asks a sharp question. Replies on your own thread keep
it in the algorithm longer.

### 4. Builder-in-public posts outperform polished announcements
"Shipped the algorithmic pace-zone math today. Here's the
formula derivation and what it changed in the recommendation
output." — wins.

"We're proud to announce a major update to MyRX!" — loses.

The first reads like the founder writing; the second reads
like a press release. The 2026 X audience filters press
releases instantly.

### 5. Reference accounts to be in conversation with
The audience that converts for both MyRX arms (coaches + serious
athletes) reads:

- **@gregnuckols** — Stronger by Science
- **@DrAndyGalpin** — performance science
- **@helms3dmj** — natural bodybuilding science
- **@BiolayneNorton** — protein / metabolism research
- **@hubermanlab** — neuroscience-adjacent (broader audience)
- **@AlanFlanagan_** — nutrition mechanism
- **@martinharrisson** — endurance / pace zones
- **@FastTalkLabs** — endurance coaching infrastructure

Reply substantively on their threads when the topic matches.
Don't tag them in original posts asking for visibility — that
reads as opportunistic and gets ignored.

### 6. Visuals on hooks only
A single image on tweet 1 of a thread outperforms a visual on
every tweet. Charts, formulas, side-by-side comparisons land
hard. Stock fitness imagery or pure decoration loses. The 2026
X audience treats visual chrome as a signal that the substance
is thin.

### 7. Never pull voice down for the algorithm
The voice that wins on X long-term is the voice that doesn't
shift. Mechanism > motivation. Specific > vague. Lowercase
coach / athlete in body. Em-dash for breaks. No emojis except
when the platform conversation calls for one (rare on X).

## What NOT to do on X

- Don't use the brand handle as the primary voice. Founder-led
  is non-negotiable for a 1-2 person team in 2026.
- Don't post the same hook on multiple platforms verbatim. X
  rewards tighter, faster, more contrarian — even when the
  underlying mechanism is identical to a LinkedIn post.
- Don't engagement-bait ("agree?" "thoughts?" "RT if you
  agree"). Suppressed.
- Don't post screenshots of LinkedIn / Threads posts. Reads as
  cross-platform spam.
- Don't tag big accounts in originals. Reply to them on their
  posts instead.
- Don't run scheduled-post-only days. Live presence (real-time
  replies, real-time observations) is what makes the account
  feel like a human builder, not a content machine.
- Don't apologize for not posting. The audience that matters
  doesn't notice gaps; the audience that notices gaps isn't
  the audience that buys.
