## Recommended primary handle

**`@teammyrx`**

One handle, every platform. Reads as a brand with a team behind it (coaches, algorithm, company), fits the B2B2C model where coaches are first-class, and avoids the two confirmed conflict clusters (RxFit on the fitness side, MyRx healthcare brands on the adjacent side).

`@myrxfit` looks like the obvious choice because it matches our domain `myrxfit.com`. It isn't. A competing fitness brand called RxFit already holds that handle on Instagram, LinkedIn, and Pinterest ‚Äî the three platforms where fitness brand discovery actually happens. Claiming `@myrxfit` on the remaining platforms would force a user searching "myrxfit" to land on RxFit first on the platforms that matter most.

## Confirmed conflicts to know about

### RxFit (`@myrxfit` on IG, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Facebook)

A direct fitness-coaching competitor. Their LinkedIn page lists `myrxfit.com` as their company website ‚Äî a domain that's actually registered to us, not them. They likely listed an aspirational URL on a marketing template. Messy but not blocking: we own the domain, they own the social handles. The practical implication for our content team is that we must drive users to `myrxfit.com` and `@teammyrx`, never to "search MyRXFit on Instagram."

### MyRx (India) ‚Äî `@myrx` on LinkedIn

Online retail healthcare platform connecting consumers with chemists, labs, doctors, hospitals. Founded 2014, partnership entity, website `myrx.co.in`. Different industry, but search-adjacent. Low confusion risk for the athlete audience; small confusion risk for press or investors searching "MyRx" + "healthcare."

### MyRxProfile, MyRxPharmacy, MyRxCare, MyRxHealth

A cluster of small healthcare brands using the `myrx*` prefix. None directly compete with us, but they crowd the search-namespace around the bare word "myrx." This is another reason `teammyrx` reads more cleanly than bare `myrx`.

### RxMuscle, FitRx, MyFitRx

Loose-association competitors in the broader Rx-fitness namespace. Low confusion risk individually, but search engines may surface them on broad "rx fit" queries.

This research is for naming decisions, not for action. Before any handle gets claimed:

1. Log in to the platform manually.
2. Use the platform's NATIVE search/handle-availability UI to confirm the handle is actually registerable.
3. Search the platform's user search for "teammyrx," "myrx," and "team myrx" to surface any active accounts the URL probe missed.

The five platforms that returned ambiguous data (TikTok, X, Threads, Reddit, Facebook) all require manual verification regardless of which handle we pick.
